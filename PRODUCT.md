# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

SRE/DevOps hiring managers and technical recruiters. They are evaluating a candidate - reading quickly, forming a judgment about capability, work ethic, and fit. They may be skimming, clicking around, or reading in depth. The interface should reward all three modes.

## Product Purpose

A publicly deployed personal portfolio and homelab showcase. The site is simultaneously the artifact being presented and the demonstration of the capability behind it. It houses a blog documenting the homelab build journey, an About Me page functioning as a live cover letter, a Resume page, a working Weather App, and live Grafana observability - all wired together from scratch by one person.

Success looks like a visitor who wasn't bored, understood what Cliff is capable of, and had no friction finding what they needed.

## Brand Personality

Engaging, informative, scrappy. Self-taught and earned - not overproduced. Direct and confident without being polished for polish's sake. The personality of an engineer who figured things out by doing them, not by reading about them.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated aesthetics: over-templated, too-smooth, interchangeable developer portfolio look
- Jarring high-contrast color combinations (neon on black, highlighter yellow combos)
- Corporate stiffness or enterprise UI language
- Flashy developer portfolio theatrics that prioritize spectacle over signal

## Design Principles

1. **Earned, not decorated** - every visual choice should feel placed with intent, not sprinkled on. The same discipline that went into the infrastructure goes into the design.
2. **Content is the product** - typography, layout, and interaction exist to surface Cliff's story and capability, not to perform design skill.
3. **Scrappy intelligence** - sharp and capable, not overproduced. Should not look like it cost a fortune or look like it was free.
4. **Purposeful interaction** - dynamic elements and animations should reveal information or aid comprehension, never decorate for their own sake.
5. **Accessible by default** - dark mode, color-blind-friendly palette, and reduced motion support are built in, not bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Color-blind-friendly palette required. Dark mode support via `prefers-color-scheme`. Reduced motion preference respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Scope: `weather-app/` directory only.
