Homelab Project Blog

Follow my journey building a modern DevOps homelab from scratch — using Terraform, Ansible, Docker, and Kubernetes with observability tooling like Grafana, Prometheus, and Elastic.

Phase 1: Intitial Lab Setup

Completed on May 25, 2025

Finished setting up Proxmox, Ubuntu/AlmaLinux templates, internal DNS/DHCP, Wazuh, Grafana, and Prometheus. Grafana, Prometheus, and Wazuh exist in the lab on dedicated machines as well as containerized on a single machine. This phase was an exercise in understanding the quirks of Proxmox and subsequently qemu, virtual networking, and VM templating.
Many lessons were learned. How to use dnsmasq properly, the quirks of virtual networking and drivers that are shipped with .isos are chiefe among them. IaC, especially Terraform, was used heavily when templating and testing deployments.
Technology Used: VSCode, Proxmox, Rufus, Wazuh, Prometheus, Node Exporter, Grafana, Terraform, Ansible, CMD, SSH, PowerShell, Bash.

Phase 2: Get Hands Dirty (IaC Automation)

Completed on June 4, 2025

Terraform served a dual purpose in this phase. The first was during VM Templated, mostly when dealing with Cloud-Init's quirks. It was also used in conjuction with Ansible to deploy a very barebones LAMP Stack. Prepped for cloud-init-based hostname tagging.
Another lesson learned in the virtualization world - some OSes are too new to leverage legacy storage controllers and so it must be set in main.tf or you will have a bad time.
Technology Used: VSCode, Proxmox, Terraform (Telmate 3.0.1-rc9), Ansible, cloud-init, dnsmasq, CMD, PowerShell, Bash, SSH, nmcli.
